Is it all a dream? Just some place you imagined?

Not at all. It's New Zealand.

Embracing the title "home of Middle-earth" from Lord of the Rings, New Zealand operators have set up tours to take you out of this world, and visit locations where the movies were shot.

If you want see a bit of culture, the Maori people's history, language and traditions are central to New Zealand's identity. You can travel to tribal meeting grounds to see traditions performed firsthand.

In Auckland you can visit the Sky Tower and literally walk on the wild side. One thousand feet off the ground there's a 4-foot wide walkway with no rails where you can walk around the outside of the tower. A safety harness is included, but it is not for the faint of heart.

Want luxury and pampering? Stop in to the WaiOra Resort and experience a volcanic stone, or a Miri Miri massage. The Miri Miri massage is based on Maori massage techniques used for over 800 years.

AN UNCOMMON EXPERIENCE

If all of this sounds good to you and you value a bit of exclusivity, consider this. Advertised airfare to New Zealand runs about $1,200 per person. Hotels run about $400 per night. Have a $15 egg breakfast, and that doesn't include a beverage. Vacation packages easily run $5,000 per person.

You won't see any Disney-like crowds here. It's premium pricing, without the artificially high gouging.

Things really are expensive at the end of the civilized world.
